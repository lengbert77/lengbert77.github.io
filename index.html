About Me

As a lifetime resident of the beautiful state of Virginia, I have spent most of my life living either in or around the city of Fredericksburg. I currently reside in Fredericksburg with my wife, and work for the Prince William County Service Authority, in addition to attending online grad classes at Old Dominion University (ODU). I completed my undergrad at the University of Mary Washington (UMW), and have also taken several non-degree classes through Northern Virginia Community College and Germanna. Some topics that I have especially enjoyed studying and putting into practice professionally are Python programming and spatial analysis.

Some of my personal interests include biking (I once completed a "Century ride", which was 100 miles in one day), weightlifting, science fiction (especially Star Wars, Star Trek, and Dr. Who), and outdoor activities of all kinds. I also enjoy all kinds of music from country to classical, and everything in between. I have been playing the violin for close to 20 years, in addition to having participated in various choirs and choruses throughout the years. I am also an active volunteer with my wife as a leader for the youth group at our local church.

Education and Work Experience

As an undergraduate, I majored in Geography, minored in Data Science, and also earned a certificate in GIS. My undergrad background in Data Science is what led me to begin pursuing my M.S. in Data Science and Analytics. Professionally, I have now been working almost 3 years for the Prince William County Service Authority as a GIS Technician

  • Graduated from University of Mary Washington in Spring 2020
  • Worked as a VA State Park Ranger from 2015-2020
  • Studied Geography, GIS, and Data Science
  • Work as a GIS Data Technician at the Prince William County Service Authority
  • Pursuing M.S. in Data Science and Analytics at Old Dominion University
